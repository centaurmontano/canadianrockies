Canmore Kananaskis, located in Alberta, Canada, is a stunning region nestled within the Canadian Rockies. It covers a vast and picturesque area known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. The region is home to majestic mountains, pristine lakes, lush forests, and a rich history dating back to the early 19th century.

Where did Canmore get its name?

The town of Canmore in Alberta, Canada, got its name from Malcolm III of Scotland, who is also known as Malcolm Canmore. "Canmore" is derived from the Gaelic word "ceann mòr," which means "big head" or "great chief." Malcolm III of Scotland was a Scottish king who reigned from 1058 to 1093. The town was named in the late 19th century by a group of Canadian Pacific Railway officials who were of Scottish descent and chose to name the town after Malcolm Canmore as a nod to their Scottish heritage.

History

  • Early 1800s - The area now known as Canmore Kananaskis was explored by fur traders and early pioneers.
  • 1880s - Coal mining played a significant role in the region's development, attracting settlers and workers.
  • 1960s - Canmore transitioned from a mining town to a vibrant mountain community.
  • 1988 - Canmore hosted the Nordic events of the Calgary Winter Olympics.
  • Today - Canmore Kananaskis is a beloved destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering hiking, biking, skiing, and more.

What to expect?

Canmore Kananaskis is a paradise for nature enthusiasts. Here, you can embark on incredible hikes through the rugged terrain, explore biking trails that offer thrilling adventures, and paddle in the crystal-clear waters of pristine lakes. If you prefer a more leisurely experience, you can take scenic drives, enjoy picnics with breathtaking mountain views, and observe the abundant wildlife. In the winter, the region transforms into a wonderland, offering activities like snowshoeing, skiing, fat biking, ice skating, and much more!

Exploring Downtown Canmore and Nearby Attractions


Town of Canmore

Three Sisters Mountain

Canmore Bridge


Begin your Canmore adventure in the heart of downtown, where you'll find charming shops, cozy cafes, and a vibrant atmosphere. From here, you can explore the following attractions:

  • Canmore Nordic Centre - Experience world-class cross-country skiing and biathlon facilities.
    Approx. 10 minutes (6 km) from downtown.
  • Rat's Nest Cave - Explore the mysterious underground world with guided tours.
    Approx. 20 minutes (15 km) from downtown.
  • Grassi Lakes - Hike the scenic Grassi Lakes Trail, starting right from downtown, and be rewarded with stunning turquoise lakes surrounded by lush forests.
    Accessible from downtown.
  • Policeman's Creek Boardwalk - Enjoy a leisurely stroll along Policeman's Creek Boardwalk, a serene path that winds through picturesque natural beauty within walking distance of downtown.
    Located in downtown Canmore.
  • Trails and Three Sisters Mountain - Discover a network of scenic hiking and biking trails, with stunning views of the iconic Three Sisters Mountain.
    Easily accessible from downtown.
  • Canmore Legacy Pavilion - Explore exhibits showcasing the town's rich history and culture.
    Located in Canmore downtown.
